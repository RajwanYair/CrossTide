## Overview

The **Correlation Matrix** card shows an n×n heatmap of Pearson correlation coefficients
between every pair of tickers in your watchlist. It helps identify diversification gaps
and over-concentrated positions.

<Aside type="caution">
  Correlation is computed from historical returns and can change rapidly during market
  stress events (correlations tend to spike toward 1.0 in crashes).
</Aside>

## How It Works

1. Fetches daily close prices for all watchlist tickers
2. Computes pairwise Pearson-r correlation over the selected lookback window
3. Renders an interactive heatmap with color-coded cells

## Reading the Heatmap

| Correlation (r) | Color       | Meaning                               |
| --------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------- |
| +0.85 to +1.00  | Deep red    | Highly correlated — over-concentration risk |
| +0.50 to +0.85  | Orange      | Moderate positive correlation         |
| −0.20 to +0.50  | White/gray  | Low correlation — good diversification |
| −0.50 to −0.20  | Light blue  | Mild negative — some hedging benefit  |
| −1.00 to −0.50  | Deep blue   | Strong inverse — effective hedge      |

## Controls

### Period Selector

Choose the lookback window:

- **20 days** — Recent short-term correlation (≈1 month)
- **60 days** — Medium-term (≈1 quarter)
- **120 days** — Long-term structure (≈6 months)

Shorter windows capture regime changes faster but are noisier.

### Exclude Crypto Toggle

Cryptocurrency pairs often have different correlation dynamics than equities.
Toggle this to focus on stock-to-stock relationships only.

## Over-Concentration Warnings

Pairs with |r| > 0.85 are highlighted with a warning indicator. If your portfolio
holds multiple highly correlated assets, you have concentrated directional risk —
a decline in one is likely mirrored by the other.

**Example:** Holding both MSFT and AAPL (historically r ≈ 0.7–0.8) provides less
diversification than holding MSFT + XOM (historically r ≈ 0.3).

## Practical Tips

- A well-diversified watchlist typically has an **average pairwise correlation < 0.5**
- Consider adding assets from different sectors, geographies, or asset classes
- Use the 120-day window for strategic allocation decisions
- Use the 20-day window to spot short-term regime shifts
