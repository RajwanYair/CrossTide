## Overview

The **Macro Dashboard** card tracks five key macro-economic indicators that influence
equity markets. It also derives an automatic **risk regime** badge to quickly assess
the current market environment.

## Indicators Tracked

| Label  | Ticker   | Description                                         |
| ------ | -------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| VIX    | ^VIX     | CBOE Volatility Index ("fear gauge")                |
| US 10Y | ^TNX     | 10-Year US Treasury yield                           |
| DXY    | DX-Y.NYB | US Dollar Index (strength of USD vs basket)        |
| Gold   | GC=F     | Gold futures (safe-haven asset)                     |
| WTI    | CL=F     | West Texas Intermediate crude oil futures           |

Each indicator shows:
- Current value
- Daily change and percentage
- 30-day sparkline trend

## Risk Regime Badge

The card automatically classifies the current macro environment:

| Regime       | Condition                              | Implication               |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Risk-On**  | VIX < 16 and DXY weakening            | Favorable for equities    |
| **Risk-Off** | VIX ≥ 22 or DXY strengthening > 0.35% | Capital fleeing to safety |
| **Neutral**  | Neither extreme                        | Mixed signals             |

<Aside type="tip">
  Use the risk regime badge as a quick filter: avoid adding new long positions
  during sustained "Risk-Off" periods. The macro backdrop often overrides individual
  stock signals.
</Aside>

## Cross-Asset Relationships

Understanding how these indicators interact:

| Relationship             | Typical Pattern                              |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| VIX ↑ + Equities ↓      | Fear rising → sell pressure                  |
| 10Y ↑ + Growth stocks ↓ | Higher discount rate hurts long-duration assets |
| DXY ↑ + Gold ↓          | Strong dollar weighs on commodities          |
| Gold ↑ + VIX ↑          | Flight to safety during uncertainty          |
| WTI ↑ + Energy ↑        | Oil bull benefits energy sector              |

## Interpreting Changes

### VIX Levels

- **< 14:** Extreme complacency (historically precedes spikes)
- **14–20:** Normal conditions
- **20–30:** Elevated fear
- **> 30:** Crisis/panic levels

### 10-Year Yield Context

Rising yields compress P/E multiples for growth stocks while benefiting financials.
Watch the *rate of change* more than the absolute level.

### Dollar Strength

A strong DXY headwind affects:
- US multinational earnings (negative FX translation)
- Emerging markets (dollar-denominated debt pressure)
- Commodities priced in USD (inverse relationship)
