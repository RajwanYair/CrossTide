## Overview

The **Market Breadth** card provides a bird's-eye view of your watchlist's aggregate
health. Instead of analyzing individual tickers, it answers the question: *"Is the
majority of my universe trending up or down?"*

<Aside type="tip">
  Market breadth is a leading indicator. Narrowing breadth (few stocks driving gains)
  often precedes market corrections — even when headline indices look strong.
</Aside>

## Panels

### (a) Consensus Distribution Donut

A donut chart showing the count of BUY / NEUTRAL / SELL signals across all watchlist
tickers from the CrossTide consensus engine.

- **Healthy market:** 60%+ BUY signals
- **Caution:** Roughly equal BUY and SELL counts
- **Bearish:** 50%+ SELL signals

### (b) SMA Participation

Shows what percentage of your watchlist is trading **above** key moving averages:

| Indicator       | Interpretation                                      |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Above 50-day SMA  | Short-term bullish participation                  |
| Above 200-day SMA | Long-term trend health                            |

When both readings are > 70%, the market is broadly trending. When they diverge
(50-day high, 200-day low), a short-term bounce may be fragile.

### (c) Advance / Decline Bar

A horizontal bar showing today's session:

- **Green:** Advancing stocks (positive daily change)
- **Red:** Declining stocks (negative daily change)
- **Gray:** Unchanged

A strong market day has 3:1 or better advance/decline ratio.

### (d) Top Movers & Laggards

Lists the top 3 gainers and top 3 losers from your watchlist by percentage change.
Useful for quickly spotting outlier moves that warrant investigation.

## Interpreting Breadth Divergence

When a major index (e.g., S&P 500) makes new highs but breadth indicators weaken,
this is called a **breadth divergence** — a classic warning signal:

1. Fewer stocks participating in the rally
2. Gains concentrated in a few mega-caps
3. Increased risk of mean reversion

## Data Source

Market breadth is computed from your **active watchlist** data — no additional API
calls are needed. Data refreshes automatically with each polling cycle.
